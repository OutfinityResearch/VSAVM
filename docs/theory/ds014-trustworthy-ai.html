Trust and transparency

Trustworthy behavior is a product of constrained emission and visible execution. The system is not trusted because it is confident, but because it can show the bounded search that supports its answers.

Explanations are operational, derived from the execution log and budget parameters. This turns explainability into an audit trail rather than a narrative guess.

The combination of canonical facts, bounded closure, and scoped contexts keeps the system aligned with its consistency promise. When uncertainty remains, it is surfaced explicitly rather than hidden behind fluent text.

VSAVM is an Axiologic Research experiment within the Achilles project. This static documentation is written in clear academic English for engineers.
