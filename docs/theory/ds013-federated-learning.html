Federated growth of modules

A modular architecture is well suited to federated learning because it allows sharing discrete artifacts rather than raw data. Clients can contribute counts, prototypes, and utility metrics without exposing source material.

Schema prototypes can be merged through VSA bundling, and duplicate rules can be detected by similarity and by operational effect. This supports gradual improvement rather than wholesale retraining.

The consistency contract remains central in a federated setting. New rules are accepted only after they pass health checks that limit inconsistency and uncontrolled branching.

VSAVM is an Axiologic Research experiment within the Achilles project. This static documentation is written in clear academic English for engineers.
